---
term: "P vs NP Problem"
category: "Math & Theory"
level: "Advanced"
---

P: problems solvable quickly. NP: problems verifiable quickly. Does P = NP? Can every problem whose solution is quickly verifiable also be quickly solved? Unsolved — $1M prize.

### Example

Sudoku: solving is hard (NP), but checking a solved puzzle is easy (P). If P=NP, we could solve Sudoku as fast as we check it. Most believe P≠NP.
