---
term: "Concurrency vs Parallelism"
category: "Operating Systems"
level: "Intermediate"
---

Concurrency: managing multiple tasks at once (may be interleaved on one CPU). Parallelism: actually executing multiple tasks simultaneously on multiple CPUs.

### Example

Concurrency: a chef switching between chopping and stirring (one person, multiple tasks). Parallelism: two chefs each doing a different task simultaneously.
