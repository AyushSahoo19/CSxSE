term: "Logarithm"

---

The inverse of exponentiation. log₂(N) answers: 'how many times do I halve N to reach 1?' Appears constantly in algorithm analysis.

### Example

log₂(1024) = 10. Binary search of 1024 items takes at most 10 comparisons. That's why O(log n) algorithms are so fast — doubling data only adds 1 step.
