---
term: "IP Address (IPv4 / IPv6)"
category: "Networking & Web"
level: "Foundational"
---

A unique numerical address for each device on a network. IPv4: 32-bit (192.168.1.1, ~4.3 billion addresses). IPv6: 128-bit (practically unlimited).

### Example

IPv4 addresses ran out — that's why we need IPv6. 192.168.1.1 is your home router's address (private). Your ISP assigns a public IP.
